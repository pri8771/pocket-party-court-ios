# B2B: Verdict Room (pitch + target list)

Written now, built later. A consumer party game won't pay rent; a work-safe
icebreaker product can. Do **not** build the web wedge until the consumer loop
passes the cold play-test gate (`Beta_Playtest_Plan.md`). The same engine powers
both, but the channel and UX differ enough that the web room is its own
validation lane, not a hidden feature in the iOS app.

## The wedge
A work-safe, web/meeting-friendly "Verdict Room": a manager opens a room, picks
a work-safe case ("Most likely to reply-all by accident"), the team votes, laughs,
done — in or alongside a standup/offsite. Reuses `GameStore` + `VerdictEngine`;
the Office Chaos Court deck is the seed content.

## Buyer & pain
- **Buyer:** team leads / People-ops at remote-heavy SMBs (20–200 people). Not enterprise HR (too slow).
- **Pain:** weekly standups and offsites need a 5-minute icebreaker that isn't cringe; managers improvise badly every time.
- **Why they buy:** corporate team-building runs $10–100+/person; People-ops budget per-seat already.

## Pricing
- $5–8 / seat / month team plan, or **$99 / team flat**. Per-seat is what they already budget against.

## Smallest buildable wedge
- A curated work-safe deck + a shareable web/Zoom-friendly "Verdict Room" — no native-app dependency.
- Buildable fast on the existing state machine.

## Outreach
- One-liner: *"A 5-minute icebreaker your remote team won't roll their eyes at."*
- Channel: cold email / LinkedIn straight to managers and People-ops. The verdict card is the demo.

## Target list (20–30 names to build before outreach)
Seed criteria: remote-heavy SMBs, 20–200 people, an identifiable team-lead or
People-ops owner, active on LinkedIn. Categories to source from:
- Remote-first startups (YC / Indie Hackers / RemoteOK company lists).
- Agencies and consultancies with distributed teams.
- People-ops / "Head of Remote" / "Culture" titles at 20–200-person companies.
- Community managers running internal team rituals.

rubric here so the doc doesn't go stale.)

## Sequencing
Consumer ship → cold play-test gate → if it passes, build the Verdict Room MVP
and run a separate B2B validation against this list. The verdict card stays the
shared growth/demo asset for both lanes.
